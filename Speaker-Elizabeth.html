Speaker Bio

Become A Powerful Networker (Workshop) and How Far We've Climbed (Panel)

Elizabeth Yang

Elizabeth Yang is a business strategist specializing in customer experiences and leadership through relationship marketing with technology as a multiplier. She brings nearly two decades of “human psychology in business” to helping business leaders gain financial benefits from building a customer-first organization that invests in inclusive global community experiences. She shows them how to build a profitable, customer-first organization with strength-based teams and build a relationship network to achieve goals. She is a speaker, trainer, and advisor that has worked with many global leaders and brands to get seen, heard, and connected to monetize.

Elizabeth brings nearly two decades of experience at top Fortune 500 companies working with executives and leading teams to turn ideas into viable business growth across health care, education, staffing and retail. She also gives back by serving on boards and committees for many non-profit organizations.

She is the founder of Hmong Women Take on the World, a global online summit that celebrates women and girls using emerging technology, product marketing, and social media. Launched in just 6 months, the crowdfunded campaign raised $30K USD and generated 100+ curated stories, bringing together 60 partners across 8 different countries: Australia, Canada, China, Germany, Laos, Thailand, United States, and Vietnam.

She’s a Gallup Builder Profile Talents (BP10) coach with certifications in Pragmatic Marketing (PMC), Harvard’s consulting continuing education program, and BJ Fogg’s Tiny Habits for behavioral change. She’s a former PhD candidate, studying Human Factors & Ergonomics. She holds a Master’s of Science degree in Technical Communications and a Bachelor’s of Arts degree in Business Marketing & Communications.

The AAWIL Conference, organized by ASPIRE, is an interactive, one-day event aimed at growing and developing leadership skills. The program includes a keynote address, shared discussions, small group activities, networking opportunities, workshops, and training sessions.
