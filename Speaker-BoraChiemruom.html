Speaker Bio

Panel - How Far We've Climbed/p>

Bora Chiemruom

Chiemruom is the owner of Kravant Boutique in Lowell. It’s the first and only Khmer woman-owned rental and consignment boutique in the city of Lowell, MA. As a social enterprise, Kravant boutique lends out dresses to under-resourced young women, who can’t afford the rental fees.

Previously, she served as executive director of the Asian American Commission (AAC) from 2015 to 2017, overseeing day-to-day operations and management. Since 2015, she has served as an advisory board member for the Institute for Asian American Studies. She has spoken at the National Asian American Conference on Law and Public Policy at Harvard Law School. In 2016 and 2017, she co-chaired the Asian-Americans Pacific Islanders Civil Rights Forum in Boston. Chiemruom received her bachelor’s degree in history from Fitchburg State University in 1998 and attended graduate school at Lesley University.

In January 2019, she was appointed as a Commissioner to the AAC by the State Auditor Suzanne Bump to serve a 3-year term (2019-2021). The AAC, established in 2006, meets monthly with the goal of recognizing and highlighting the many contributions of Asian Americans to the social, cultural, economic, and political life of the Commonwealth, and to identify and address the needs and challenges facing Asian Americans in Massachusetts.

As a refugee from Cambodia, she continues to be a passionate advocate for the Khmer Community and for all Asian Americans and hope to inspire the next generation to become leaders.

The AAWIL Conference, organized by ASPIRE, is an interactive, one-day event aimed at growing and developing leadership skills. The program includes a keynote address, shared discussions, small group activities, networking opportunities, workshops, and training sessions.
